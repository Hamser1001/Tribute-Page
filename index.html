  • Law of Universal Gravitation (1687)

    Newton formulated that every mass attracts every other mass with a force proportional to their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

  • Three Laws of Motion (1687)

    Established the fundamental principles of motion, laying the foundation for classical mechanics.

  • Calculus (Late 1660s – Published 1704)

    Independently developed the mathematical method of calculus, essential for describing changing quantities in physics.

  • Reflecting Telescope (1668)

    Invented the first practical reflecting telescope, improving image quality by using mirrors instead of lenses.

  • Work on Optics (1672–1704)/h4>

    Studied light and color, showing that white light is made of a spectrum of colors using prisms.

  • Principia Mathematica (1687)

    His groundbreaking book where he presented his laws of motion, universal gravitation, and laid the foundation for modern physics.
